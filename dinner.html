Nigerian Dinner

Jollof Rice

What is Jollof Rice?

Jollof Rice is a traditional African rice dish that has a red/orange color and can be served with different sides. Different countries in Africa make their own jollof rice in different ways, but Nigerian Jollof rice can have smaller components added. The tomatoes and pepper blend (the more pepper you add, the spicier it will get!) makes the spices pop.

How to cook Jollof Rice

Ingredients

Vegetable oil
3 cups of rice
½ of Butter
6 Tomatoes
3 small peppers
1 bell pepper
2 big onions
Tomato paste
Powdered Cray fish

Seasonings:
Curry
Onion
Garlic
Adobo
Thyme
Bay Leaves
Maggi Cube
Adobo seasoning
Sazon Goya
Powdered Cray Fish

Instructions

1. First blend your 6 Tomatoes, 3 peppers, bell pepper, and 1 big onion together and boil on stove until water is dried out.
2. Boil 1 cup of water and add seasonings (Onion, garlic, thyme, adobo, maggi cubes, curry) and cook for 10 minutes on high for chicken broth
3. Parboil rice for 10 minutes on high and wash and drain rice
4. Chop ½ of the other onion any way you like
5. Take a deep pot and fill vegetable oil until the bottom of the pot is filled, and wait for the oil to be hot (this can be checked if one piece of onion is sizzling)
6. Take your boiled pepper mixture and throw in oil and leave to cook for 5 minutes
7.Put in ½ tomato paste into pot and let the paste cook 2 minutes
8. Add 1 tbsp. of curry, 1 tbsp. of crayfish, 3 maggi cubes, and add ¼ cup of water
9. Let mixture simmer for 5 minutes and stir
10. Throw in 1 cup of chicken broth, 1 pack of sazon goya, and add 4 bay leaves
11. Add shrimp and mixed vegetables (optional)
12. Once seasonings are tasty enough, add your rice and stir
13. Wait 15 minutes and add ½ cup of butter
14. Wait 20 more minutes or wait until rice is soft, and you serve!

Jamaican Dinner

Jamaican Curry

What is Jamaican Curry?

The sauce is thick and rich, so this curry is comforting on a chilly evening if that when you are craving it, but the spices also work surprisingly well on even the hottest summer day. I suspect that why curry is so popular in countries known for their warm climates, like India and the Caribbean.

How to cook Jamaican Curry

Ingredients

boneless, skinless chicken
teaspoon kosher salt
olive oil
medium yellow onion
red bell pepper
jalapenos
cloves garlic
ginger
curry powdered
turmeric
allspice
cayenne peppers
potatoes
coconut milk
Worcestershire sauce
white wine vinegar
Chopped fresh cilantro
Prepared brown rice

Instructions

1. Sprinkle the chicken with salt. Set aside
2. In a Dutch oven or similar deep, sturdy pot, heat the oil over medium. Once it is hot, add the onions, and cook, stirring occasionally, until the onions begin to soften and turn translucent, 5 to 8 minutes.
3. Stir in the red bell pepper, jalapeños, garlic, and ginger. Cook, stirring often, for 2 minutes.
4.Add curry powder, turmeric, allspice, and cayenne. Cooking, stirring constantly, until spices turn deep gold and become ultra fragrant, about 1 minute.
5. Add the chicken and sauté for 5 minutes, stirring often. It should look golden on the outside but does not need to be completely cooked through.
6. Add the potatoes. Cook, stirring often, for 3 minutes.
7. Add the coconut milk, Worcestershire, vinegar, and hot sauce. Stir to combine. Bring to a simmer. Continue to simmer, reducing the heat to low as needed, until the chicken is tender and cooked through, the potatoes are tender, and sauce has slightly reduced, 15 to 20 minutes. Stir every few minutes to keep the sauce from sticking.
8. Taste and season with additional salt or hot sauce as desired. Serve hot over rice, with a big sprinkle of cilantro.
